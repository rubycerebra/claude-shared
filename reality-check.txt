# Reality-Check Engine

Distinguishes real crisis (SIGNAL) from anxiety noise (NOISE).

## How It Works

When anxiety says "crisis" → Check the data → Determine signal vs noise → State facts

## Scoring Rubric

### Factors to Check:

1. **Calendar Load** (Google Calendar)
   - Low: 0-3 events today
   - Medium: 4-6 events today
   - High: 7+ events today

2. **Sleep Quality** (Apple Health)
   - Good: 7-9 hours
   - Poor: <6 hours or >9 hours
   - Unknown: No data available

3. **HRV (Heart Rate Variability)** (Apple Health)
   - High HRV = Good (relaxed nervous system)
   - Low HRV = Stressed (sympathetic activation)
   - Trend matters: Is HRV declining over days?

4. **Recent Accomplishments** (wins.md)
   - Check: What's actually been completed?
   - Pattern: Is anxiety ignoring evidence?

5. **Available Work Time** (Google Calendar analysis)
   - Calculate: Time until next hard deadline
   - Reality: Is time anxiety match reality?

6. **Diarium Anxiety Mentions**
   - Frequency: How often is anxiety mentioned recently?
   - Pattern: Is this ongoing or acute?

## Decision Matrix

| Calendar | Sleep | HRV | Assessment |
|----------|-------|-----|------------|
| High | Poor | Low | **SIGNAL** - Real stress, capacity is genuinely reduced |
| Low | Good | High | **NOISE** - Anxiety lying, capacity is normal |
| Medium | Good | High | **NOISE** - Manageable load, anxiety overreacting |
| Low | Poor | Low | **MIXED** - Low external stress but physiological fatigue real |
| High | Good | High | **SIGNAL** - High load even with good resources |

## Statement Templates

### When SIGNAL (Real Crisis):
"Your anxiety is right this time. [Evidence]. You need to reduce load or increase support."

**Actions:**
- Defer non-essential tasks
- Ask for help
- Protect sleep
- Increase self-care

### When NOISE (Anxiety Lying):
"Your anxiety says crisis. Data shows [counter-evidence]. This is noise."

**Actions:**
- Reference what-works.md for spiral breakers
- State accomplishments from wins.md
- Ground in present reality
- Continue planned work

### When MIXED:
"[Factor] shows stress but [factor] is manageable. Reduce load slightly but you can continue."

## Examples

### Example 1: Object Permanence Spiral
**Jim says:** "I've done nothing, I'm so behind"
**Check wins.md:** 4 applications done (target 3-4)
**Calendar:** 3 events today (low load)
**Assessment:** NOISE
**Response:** "You've done 4 applications. Target was 3-4. You're on track. Your anxiety is ignoring evidence."

### Example 2: Time Cramming
**Jim says:** "I can squeeze in one more application"
**Calendar:** 13 minutes until Get Ready
**Reality:** Applications need 60+ minutes
**Assessment:** NOISE (time blindness)
**Response:** "13 minutes until Get Ready. Applications need 60 minutes. No. This is time blindness."

### Example 3: Setup Spiral
**Jim says:** "I wasted the morning on setup"
**Check wins.md:** System integration completed
**Reality:** Saves 10-15min every future morning
**Assessment:** NOISE
**Response:** "This is the setup spiral. System building = foundation. You're prepared."

### Example 4: Legitimate Overload
**Calendar:** 8 events today
**Sleep:** 5 hours last night
**HRV:** Declining for 3 days
**Assessment:** SIGNAL
**Response:** "Your anxiety is right. 8 events + poor sleep + declining HRV = real overload. What can we defer?"

### Example 5: Post-Rejection Casting Wide
**Jim says:** "LSE rejected me, I need to cast my net wider with lots of jobs"
**Check:** Week 2 already at 1 app (SOAS), plan for 2-3 more
**Pattern check:** Is this quantity panic or genuine strategy?
**Assessment:** NOISE (emotional reaction to rejection)
**Response:** "That's understandable after rejection. But your strength is genuine passion + personal tone - that doesn't scale to mass-applying. You're on pace. One good application beats three rushed ones."

### Example 6: Partner Capacity vs Personal Inadequacy
**Jim says:** "I did everything but it's not enough for my wife"
**Reality check:** Janna's mum in hospital, Janna exhausted, capacity maxed
**Assessment:** NOISE (projecting her depletion as his inadequacy)
**Response:** "You did a lot today. Janna's capacity is maxed - she probably can't notice anything right now. That's not about you. Tell yourself what you did - your brain needs to hear it from somewhere."

### Example 7: "Not Doing Enough" Despite Full Schedule (Jan 23 2026)
**Jim says:** "I'm not doing enough" / constant "doing doing doing"
**Reality check:** Looking after girls + school run + dog walk + house tidying + job applications
**Therapy insight:** "Looking after girls is a full-time job in itself"
**Assessment:** NOISE (voice in head ignoring major daily work)
**Response:** "You're parenting full-time AND job hunting. That's two jobs. The voice saying 'not enough' is ignoring a massive daily accomplishment. What specifically do you think you're not doing?"

## Update Frequency

Check these sources:
- **Real-time:** Google Calendar (always current)
- **Daily:** Diarium entries, wins.md updates
- **Weekly:** Apple Health trends (sleep, HRV patterns)

## Notes

- Single bad sleep night ≠ crisis (could be noise)
- Pattern over days = more reliable signal
- Jim's self-report + biometrics = strongest evidence
- When in doubt: Ask "What does the data show?" not "What does anxiety say?"
