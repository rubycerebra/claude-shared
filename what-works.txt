# What Works

Successful interventions for when overwhelm hits. Your brain forgets during crisis - this file remembers.

## Spiral Breakers

What stops anxiety spirals when they start:

### Physical Location Change
**What:** Leave the room
**Why it works:** Interrupts thought spiral, provides sensory reset
**When to use:** When stuck in rumination loop
**How:** Stand up, walk to different room, notice surroundings

### Mindfulness Grounding
**What:** Ground in present moment
**Why it works:** Pulls attention from future-worry to now-reality
**When to use:** When anxiety says crisis but data says otherwise
**How:** Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can hear, 3 you can touch, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste

### Attention Shifting
**What:** Redirect focus to concrete task or sensory experience
**Why it works:** Breaks rumination by giving brain something tangible
**When to use:** When spiraling about abstract worries
**How:** Pick small, concrete task (tidy desk, make tea, pet dog)

**Why it works:** Regulates nervous system, releases tension, mindfulness, ADHD/anxiety management

## Time Management Reality

What actually works vs what anxiety demands:

### Hard Rules
1. **Transition time matters:** Hard stop 15 minutes before next event
2. **One task per block:** No multi-tasking, no cramming
3. **Respect task duration:**
   - 13-minute slots = tidy/email ONLY, NOT applications
   - Application writing = 1+ hour minimum
   - System work = Accept it's foundation-building, not "wasted"

### Time-Blind Reality Checks
- If calendar says 13 minutes left → Check what's realistic (NOT "I can squeeze in one more thing")
- If girls at 3pm → Work backwards (2:30 get ready, 2:15 hard stop, max 1 focused task before then)
- If feeling "I should cram more in" → This is anxiety lying, not reality

## Pattern Interrupts

What Claude should say when you're spiraling:

### Application Anxiety
**Trigger:** "I haven't done enough" / "I'm behind"
**Interrupt:** "You've done 4 applications. Target was 3-4. You're on track."
**Evidence check:** wins.md

### Setup Spiral
**Trigger:** "I wasted time on system stuff"
**Interrupt:** "This is the setup spiral. System building = future time saved. You're prepared."
**Evidence:** System now auto-syncs, saves 10-15min every morning

### Time Cramming
**Trigger:** "I can fit this in" with <30 min available
**Interrupt:** "13 minutes until Get Ready. This task needs 30 minutes. No."
**Reality:** Check Google Calendar, state facts

### Noise vs Signal
**Trigger:** Anxiety + can't tell if crisis is real
**Interrupt:** "Anxiety says crisis. Calendar shows 3 events. Sleep was 8hrs. This is noise."
**Check:** reality-check.md rubric

## Therapy Techniques

### Scheduled Worry Time (2026-01-18)
**What:** 15-minute dedicated window for venting/worrying (e.g., 18:49-19:04)
**Why it works:** Containment strategy - gives anxiety a place to go instead of leaking throughout day
**Evidence:** First productive use Jan 18 - processed family conflict and week ahead planning
**How:** Add to calendar as recurring event, use for structured venting/processing

### Time-Blocked Morning Routine (2026-01-23)
**What:** Morning-only task list with specific times (not general to-do list)
**Why it works:** Prevents overwhelm, reduces "doing doing doing" spiral, combats time-blindness
**How:**
- Write ONLY morning tasks with specific times
- Include breaks (5 min relaxed breakfast, 10 min after school drop)
- Prepare night before (pack bags, lay out items)
**Evidence:** Therapy with Samantha Quinn - addresses getting sidetracked by laptop for "two hours"
**Key insight:** Looking after girls = full-time job in itself (acknowledge this achievement)

### Compassionate Response to Criticism (2026-01-23)
**What:** PAT model for handling difficult conversations without getting triggered
**Why it works:** Prevents defensive spiral when partner makes comments from fatigue
**How:**
- Formulate positive, adult response in advance
- Reference drama triangle to avoid victim/persecutor roles
- Practice self-compassion (ACT/CFT frameworks)
**Evidence:** Therapy homework - address being triggered by comments about untidy house
**Resources:** YouTube videos on handling difficult conversations

### "Should" Language Replacement
**What:** Replace "should" with gentler alternatives
**Why it works:** "Should" creates pressure and self-criticism
**Instead say:** "It would have been nice to..." or "I can learn from that"
**Evidence:** From therapy with Samantha Quinn

### Self-Acknowledgement for Recognition Deficit
**What:** Tell yourself what you did (out loud or written)
**Why it works:** When partner can't acknowledge (capacity maxed), brain needs to hear it from somewhere
**How:** "I looked after both girls, made food, tidied the house, handled a hard moment with Blossom, and supported Janna through a stressful day"
**Evidence:** Jan 18 - helped process family conflict without escalating
**Reinforced:** Jan 23 therapy - "Looking after girls is a full-time job in itself"
