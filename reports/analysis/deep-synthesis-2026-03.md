## 3. Coaching Signals

- **Notice that rest produced Friday's recovery, not effort.** Multiple weeks confirmed the same sequence: yoga Thursday evening → measurably better Friday. The instinct is to push through; the evidence says the opposite works. Consider treating Thursday evening rest as non-negotiable infrastructure rather than optional luxury.

- **Notice the gap between pattern-naming and pattern-breaking.** Metacognitive awareness increased significantly this month — real-time catching of hyperfocus, masking cost, open-loop anxiety. The next edge isn't more awareness, it's building a stop condition: a specific, pre-decided action when the pattern is spotted. "I notice I'm in the loop" could become "I notice, so I [specific thing]."

- **Consider that the PDA override worked via choice + rest, not via pressure.** The Chris email sent after a 48-hour stall, the film premiere reframed from obligation — both succeeded because the framing shifted to "I'm choosing this" rather than "I need to do this." When the next demanding task arrives, the evidence says: rest first, then reframe as choice, then act. Pressure consistently extends the stall.

- **Consider naming the uncloseable loop as a distinct category in therapy.** Manageable open loops respond to task completion. The scam loop, the ambient backlog — these bypass all coping because completion isn't available. They need a different intervention (acceptance, deliberate parking, ritual close) rather than the same productivity tools applied to closeable tasks.
