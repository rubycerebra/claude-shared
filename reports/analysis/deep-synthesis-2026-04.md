## 4. Recommendations

- **One option would be an "open loop clearance" block — 15 minutes, once or twice a week, dedicated to closing or consciously parking the small admin items that carry disproportionate neural weight.** The evidence is strong: invoice anxiety (W14), Chris Milton contracts (W15), subtitle fix (W16), Tilly presents (W18) — none were difficult tasks, but all created persistent background activation. A short, scheduled window could reduce baseline anxiety without adding demand. Even just writing "parked until [date]" on a sticky note and putting it somewhere visible might close the somatic loop.

- **Tracking the gap between sleep hours and HRV recovery could surface something useful.** W18 showed 8h sleep with 33.4ms HRV — the hours improved but the nervous system hadn't caught up. If this divergence persists, it might indicate that sleep quality (not just quantity) or daytime activation levels are the actual lever. One option: logging a simple "rested vs. unrested" feel alongside the numbers for a few weeks to see whether subjective recovery tracks hours, HRV, or something else entirely.

- **A system-level suggestion: the weekly signals extraction could flag weekend data thinness automatically.** If Saturday and Sunday entries fall below a word-count threshold (say, under 50 words combined), the weekly digest could note "weekend entries sparse — recovery or depletion unclear." This would make the blind spot visible without adding any journalling obligation. It turns a data gap into a gentle prompt rather than a demand.
