## ⚡ Electric Shower — Critical Context

**Are the £65–120 prices normal?** For mixer showers, yes — Grohe/Hansgrohe are premium brands priced for high-pressure thermostatic systems. **For an electric shower, you're probably overpaying.** Electric shower heads just need to be passive — they reshape water flow but don't restrict it. You don't need German hotel-grade engineering for that.

**Why heads have "exploded" before:** Electric shower units have a heating element that requires consistent water flow. If a head creates back-pressure (flow restrictors, EcoSmart limiters, diverter valves that partially block flow in some modes), pressure builds in the unit and can damage or rupture the element. The fix is using a head with **no flow restrictor** and **passive-only mode switching**.

**What "passive" means:** All spray modes are just different nozzle arrangements — the total water output is never blocked or restricted. No EcoSmart/water-saving limiters. No diverter valves that create dead-end pressure.

### ❌ Do NOT Use on Electric Showers
- **Hansgrohe Raindance EcoSmart versions** (model 26521000) — 9 l/min flow restrictor creates back-pressure risk
- **Any head labelled "water saving" or "EcoSmart"** without checking it's a passive restrictor (some are, some block flow)
- **Heads with "off" positions on the mode dial** — any position that stops flow entirely risks pressure buildup
- **Generic Amazon push-button multi-function heads (£10–30)** — button fails within months AND the diverter quality is unknown; back-pressure risk plus durability problem

---

**Root cause of most failures:** The push-button diverter mechanism is the #1 failure point on budget multi-function shower heads. Chinese Amazon brands use cheap plastic internals that seize, crack, or depress unevenly. The fix: either upgrade to a brand with premium button engineering (Grohe SmartTip) or switch to a **twist/dial mechanism** entirely — or just buy a Mira/Triton purpose-built electric shower head at a fraction of the price.
