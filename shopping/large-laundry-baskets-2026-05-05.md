## Cross-Cutting Warnings & Themes

### "Cheapification" of plastic (multiple users, high scores)
- **planetearthisblu** (88 points): "new ones are flimsy as fuck now"
- **Shorts_at_Dinner** (32 points): "The one I got 25 years ago is super thick plastic and looks brand new... Bought another one of the same brand 2 years ago and it's thin and flimsy. My guess is it has 1-2 more years left tops."
- **Takeaway:** Brand name alone is not sufficient — older plastic baskets were substantially better quality. Users recommending old Sterilite/Rubbermaid etc. may be describing discontinued manufacturing standards.

### UV degradation warning
- **emmaa5382** (5 points): "I think it was because I'd left it in the sun and the UV destroyed the plastic... Same thing made a bunch of my pegs super brittle."
- **Takeaway:** White/light plastic baskets degrade rapidly when left in sunlight. Non-plastic alternatives (rope, canvas, IKEA bags) are immune to this.

### Stair toboganning (humour, but implied real cause of damage)
- Top joke thread (269 points, 97 points, 25 points) suggests the OP's damage was from sliding down stairs — multiple users confirmed this is a common basket-killer.
- **_jjkase** (18 points): "My last one looked like that, but now I carry it downstairs nicely and it still looks pristine after 7 years."

### Canvas/textile as eco alternative
- **Goldie1822** (7 points): explicitly flags plastic as ocean-bound waste; recommends rope or canvas/textile as environmental alternatives.
