## Reddit Context

From r/BuyItForLife hamper thread (93 upvotes):
- Plastic hampers have collapsed in quality -- old stock still going but new ones "absolute trash", crack within a year
- Fabric/polyester hampers (53 votes): "don't make em like they used to -- polyester ones are becoming a solid alternative"
- Reddit has **no strong UK-specific consensus** on fabric bins with lids. Joseph Joseph is the only named recommendation with any traction.

## Commonly Warned Against

- **Cheap new plastic hampers** -- frame cracks, lid hinges snap. Avoid anything with moulded plastic structural parts.
- **Non-removable inner sections** -- having to tip rather than lift is awkward at scale for a family of four.

## Key Takeaways

- **Biggest capacity + removable liners:** Keplin 150L (~25-35 GBP) -- right size for family of four
- **Best from UK retailer + easy returns:** Dunelm Double (18-20 GBP) -- lower risk, physically returnable  
- **Only Reddit-named option:** Joseph Joseph Tota (60-70 GBP) -- contested but community-endorsed
- **Premium/most durable frame:** Dunelm Bamboo Double (40-42 GBP)
- **Capacity guide:** 90-150L total is right for a family of four. Below 60L fills in 2-3 days.
