# Reddit Thread: What bean to cup coffee machines should I buy?
*r/AskUK | Fetched: 2026-05-05 | Score: 0 | Comments: 14*

## Summary

OP is looking for a bean-to-cup machine in the UK with a £400 budget, wanting at least dual espresso. They've looked at Procook, Sage (too expensive), and DeLonghi. The thread is small (14 comments) but gives a clear UK consensus: DeLonghi and Melitta are the go-to brands at this budget, with Philips as a strong value pick. Sage is universally acknowledged as excellent but out of reach at £400.

## Key Themes

- **DeLonghi** — most-mentioned brand; multiple owners report 10+ years of daily use with minimal issues. Magnifica and Rivelia models cited. One commenter noted Italian-made models are preferable.
- **Melitta** — two owners report multi-year, high-volume reliability. Solo+Milk noted as budget-friendly but with trade-offs (no auto-milk, small bean hopper, no preset milk programmes). Refurb from AO via eBay recommended as a way to get one at half price.
- **Philips 2200** — flagged as excellent bang-for-buck at £250–£300. Cited positively by someone who drinks coffee from a friend's machine regularly.
- **Sage** — acknowledged as best-in-class but over the £400 budget.
- **Jura** — mentioned as the premium tier when budget is no object.
- **Maintenance** — DeLonghi owners note the brew unit needs regular cleaning and O-ring re-greasing/replacement. Not a dealbreaker but worth knowing.

## Consensus

DeLonghi is the safe, proven choice at this budget — multiple owners with 10+ years of daily use. Melitta is equally well-regarded for longevity. Philips 2200 offers the best value if budget is a priority. Refurb/eBay is actively recommended as a way to stretch the budget.

## Caveats / Disagreements

- Melitta Solo+Milk has notable trade-offs at the budget end: no auto-milk frother, no preset milk drink programmes, small bean hopper.
- £400 is described as "not a huge budget" for bean-to-cup — one commenter explicitly says there are trade-offs at this price point.
- One commenter recommends the r/superautomatic subreddit for deeper research.
- One joke comment (Heinz) — safely ignored.
